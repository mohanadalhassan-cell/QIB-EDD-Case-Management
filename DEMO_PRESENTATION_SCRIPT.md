## SLIDE 7: GOVERNANCE & COMPLIANCE (4 minutes)

### What You Say (Don't Go Back to Sections):

> "I want to talk about something that doesn't get enough attention: **governance.**
> 
> When we built this system, we made one core decision:
> 
> **'We will never calculate risk in this system. We will only READ risk from upstream systems, INVESTIGATE what we find, and DECIDE what to do.'**
> 
> Why does that matter?
> 
> Because when QCB audits us, they don't have to wonder: 'Did EDD system change the risk score incorrectly?'
> 
> The answer is: **No. We never touch the risk score. CRP calculates it. We display it. We investigate it.**
> 
> Every data block in this system has three things:
> 
> 1. **Source:** Where the data came from (T24, CRP, Regulatory DB, etc.)
> 2. **Timestamp:** When it was last updated
> 3. **Status:** Whether it's verified, pending, or expired
> 
> This is what we call **Data Source Transparency.** And it's built into every screen."

### What You Say (Continue):

> "Second governance principle: **Audit Trail.**
> 
> We capture who did what, when they did it, and where.
> 
> - Investigator decision: Captured automatically with name + timestamp
> - Manager review: Captured automatically with name + timestamp  
> - System changes: All logged
> 
> This is non-repudiation. If someone ever says 'I didn't approve this,' we have proof that they did. With their name, their time, their action.
> 
> **This is what QCB is looking for.** This is what compliance teams need.
> 
> And our system delivers it automatically."

## SLIDE 9: Q&A (5 minutes)

### Anticipated Questions & Answers:

---

### Q1: "What if CRP system goes down? What happens to EDD?"

**Answer:**
> "Good question. CRP doesn't go down often, but when it does, we have a fallback mechanism. The system will show the last known risk score and a 'Stale Data' warning. The investigator can then decide: Do we proceed with stale data, or wait for CRP to recover?
> 
> Most importantly: EDD doesn't depend on CRP being up. EDD can still run with historical data. It's not an all-or-nothing system."

---

### Q2: "Can the investigator override the risk score from CRP?"

**Answer:**
> "No. And that's intentional. The investigator cannot change CRP's risk score. But they can explain it. They can document why a HIGH RISK score is actually explainable. And based on that explanation, they can recommend APPROVE or ESCALATE.
> 
> This is the governance principle I mentioned: **We read the risk. We don't change it.** That keeps CRP and EDD separate. That keeps both systems accountable."

---

### Q3: "How long does a typical investigation take?"

**Answer:**
> "Depends on complexity. Simple cases? 20-30 minutes. Complex cases with multiple red flags? 1-2 hours. On average, we're seeing 45 minutes per case. The manager review adds another 20-30 minutes.
> 
> So total: 60-90 minutes. Compared to the old manual process of 2-3 hours. So yes, we're saving time, but more importantly, we're not sacrificing quality to get there."

---

### Q4: "Can the system handle PEP (Politically Exposed Person) cases?"

**Answer:**
> "Yes. Section 10 is specifically for PEP information. If the customer is flagged as a PEP, Section 10 becomes conditional — it appears automatically. It shows:
> 
> - Is this customer a PEP?
> - What screening databases are they checked against? (OFAC, EU, UN, etc.)
> - What's the status?
> 
> All auto-populated from our regulatory database. The investigator doesn't have to manually check OFAC. It's already done."

---

### Q5: "What about escalation process? When does a case go to Compliance?"

**Answer:**
> "Escalation happens in two scenarios:
> 
> 1. The investigator recommends ESCALATE (like in this example)
> 2. The manager rejects the investigator's recommendation
> 
> In both cases, the case gets flagged in the system, and it automatically routes to the Compliance team queue. They get a notification. They review it. They make the final call.
> 
> This is our three-tier governance: Investigator → Manager → Compliance. Each layer adds a check."

---

### Q6: "How long do you plan to support this? Is this a long-term system?"

**Answer:**
> "Yes. This is built to be the enterprise standard for EDD investigation across the bank. We're planning to integrate it with onboarding workflows, AML monitoring, and periodic review processes.
> 
> Long-term vision: Every high-risk customer gets a full EDD investigation. Periodically, we re-investigate to make sure their profile hasn't changed.
> 
> This isn't a one-time tool. It's the foundation of our enterprise AML program."
